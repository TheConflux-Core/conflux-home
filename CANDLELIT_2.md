## The Seven Themes

### 1. LIFE — The Keyword

The entire gap between "good app" and "world-changing product" is LIFE. Animation. Movement. Evidence that something intelligent is breathing behind the glass.

- Budget ring should PULSE
- Kitchen should have steam rising
- Dream mountain should have clouds drifting
- Agents should be doing something — even when you're not looking
- Every surface should breathe
- The heartbeat doesn't go away after setup — it's EVERYWHERE

### 2. The Desktop Becomes the Home/Control Room

Not a separate screen. Not a 3D pixel world. The existing desktop IS the control room, enhanced with:

- **Energy signatures** — glowing nodes, pulsing connections, flowing particles between systems
- **Status rings** — agent activity shown as animated rings, not static badges
- **Data pulsing along paths** — when an agent is working, its connection lights up, data flows
- **Ambient life** — wallpaper breathes, taskbar icons pulse, dock tiles have micro-animations

The desktop doesn't become a new thing. It becomes ALIVE.

**Rejected approach:** Claw3D-style 3D/pixel office environments. Too retro, too gimmicky, fights our design language.

**Chosen approach:** Option 4 (enhanced desktop) with elements of Options 1+2 (energy signatures, neural web). The home IS the desktop. Agents are present without being literal.

### 3. Agent Onboarding After User Onboarding

After the user sets up their account → introduce the team properly:

- "This is Helix — she researches everything."
- "This is Forge — he builds what you dream up."
- "This is Prism — she keeps the team organized."
- Make each agent a CHARACTER with a role, not just a card in a grid
- Assure the user: all agents are available, they can enable them in settings
- Chat dropdown should let you choose which agent to DM

### 4. The Main Agent = "Conflux"

- Don't name the main agent after a sub-agent
- The brand IS the companion
- Everyone talks about "their Conflux"
- Later: let users customize name, personality, identity
- Day one: the brand is the friend

### 5. Games > Stories

Stories becomes a Games library. Classic games + interactive fiction:

**Stock games (v1):**
1. **Minesweeper** — the hook, the Minesweeper moment
2. **Solitaire** — classic card game
3. **Pac-Man** — arcade classic
4. **Snake** — arcade classic
5. **Conflux Stories** — AI interactive fiction (v2 redesign, Coming Soon for now)

**Why games matter:**
- Immediate nostalgia, instant value, low build cost
- Infinite retention — people open the app to play, then stay for everything else
- Free stock games must be INCREDIBLE — if they suck, no one explores further
- Games prove the "life" concept — animated, responsive, alive
- The Minesweeper of Windows, the Chess of macOS — we need our equivalent

### 6. Bento Grid > Everything

- No more forms. No more flat tables.
- Every screen is a bento grid of living tiles
- Each tile is a world — puffs of data, pulsing meters, animated icons, micro-interactions
- Every tile should breathe
- Each app should feel like a standalone app purchased from the app store

### 7. Google Center

- Full-screen atmospheric view of their Google world
- Calendar with events, Drive with files, Gmail preview
- Not editing — VIEWING. Then enhance their ability to use it.
- Clicking mini calendar → full calendar grid popup
- New doc/sheet buttons that open in new tabs
- Lean into the CONNECTION between what Conflux does and what they can see
- Bridge: "my AI agent told me" → "I can see it right there"

| App | Identity | Feeling | Status |
|-----|----------|---------|--------|
| **Budget** | Pulse | Dark emerald, heartbeat ring, financial glow | ✅ Built — needs LIFE pass |

| **Dreams** | Horizon | Deep blue, mountain visualization, summit glow | ✅ Built — needs LIFE pass |

| **Games** | Story | Rich burgundy, parchment, candlelight | 🔨 Active — Games Hub + Minesweeper |
| **Home** | Foundation | Warm gray, blueprint lines, alert beacons | ✅ Built — needs LIFE pass |
| **Agents** | Family | Conflux purple, avatar gallery, pulse dots | ⏳ Not started |
| **Market** | Bazaar | Gold accents, card shelves, discovery animations | ⏳ Not started |
| **Google Center** | — | Atmospheric, connected, alive | ⏳ Not started |

### The Pattern — Per App LIFE Pass

1. **Hero section** — Big, visual, animated. Shows AI's understanding. Not data — VISUALIZATION OF INTELLIGENCE.
2. **AI input** — Prominent, glowing, inviting. Natural language is PRIMARY. "Tell me..." or "What do you need?"
3. **AI response** — Parsed result with animation, personality, confidence. AI talks back.
4. **Proactive insights** — Cards that appear without asking. Patterns. Trends. Nudges. "Agent watching over you" moments.
5. **Data layer** — Actual entries, history, details. Below the fold. AI surface is the experience.
6. **Bento grid** — No flat layouts. Every section is a living tile.
7. **Animations** — Every surface breathes. Pulses. Glows. Responds.
8. **Sound design** — Subtle. Click sounds. Notification chimes. The hum of a working agent.

## The Core Insight — From the First Session, Still True

> **"Does this make the user NEED this app, or merely want it?"**

Want is disposable. Want is "that's cool, I'll check it out later."

Need is: "I can't manage my money without Pulse anymore." "My kitchen agent planned my week and I didn't have to think about it." "I tried to go back to regular note-taking and it felt broken."

Need is when removing the app creates a GAP in someone's life.

**We create need through:**
1. AI as hero, not assistant
2. Each app as its own world
3. Proactive, not reactive behavior
4. Natural language as interface
5. Emotional resonance
